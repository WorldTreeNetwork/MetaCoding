# Wave-0 pilot — the full production port recipe on two fresh farmOS features (MetaCoding-9h5.25)

> Bead MetaCoding-9h5.25 · 2026-07-20 · the wave-1 dress rehearsal. Runs the
> production port recipe end-to-end on TWO fresh clean-slice features with the
> shared kernel v1 (`src/kernel/*`, 9h5.24) as fixed input, to prove the recipe
> and — the real deliverable — catalogue every friction point wave 1's automation
> must close. This is the run whose recipe wave 1 executes 100+ times.

## Bottom line

- **Two fresh features selected on graph stats:** **w0a = farm_inventory**
  (`modules/core/inventory`, 78 graph nodes, clean core module) and **w0b =
  farm_animal lifecycle** (`modules/asset/animal` 6 nodes + `modules/log/birth`
  38 nodes, boundary-adjacent scope). They were chosen to stress **maximally
  different kernel surfaces**: w0a a numeric **running-balance-with-reset** fold,
  w0b the **CM-decision registry** (birth-uniqueness is already kernel-bound) plus
  a cross-entity **lineage** fold. Neither overlaps the two already-built features
  (logs+quantities, location+movement); neither is a role/permission module.
- **The deterministic + LLM machinery (recipe stages SCOPE→SURFACE→SEMANTICS→DECIDE)
  ran cleanly on both features for $0.42 total OpenAI spend** (w0a $0.21, w0b
  $0.21 — squarely under the $3/feature abort and $6 total cap). `ctkr
  mine-fixtures` recovered the non-obvious semantics of both features
  (reset-is-assignment, done-only gating, per-(measure,units) independence;
  parent-append-if-empty, grow-only nicknames, the UniqueBirthLog hard invariant);
  `ctkr propose-adapter` generated behaviorally-faithful adapter surfaces from
  artifacts+source with no hand-authoring.
- **The single biggest finding is a KERNEL GAP, surfaced the moment two fresh
  features were tried: the shared kernel v1 ships only a latest-wins fold
  (`pickLatest`/`LwwRegister`), but 3 of the 4 headline semantics across these two
  features are NOT latest-wins** — inventory's reset/increment/decrement running
  balance, birth's parent-append-if-empty guard, and animal's grow-only nickname
  multiset. The two *already-built* features were LWW-heavy by luck (membership,
  current-location, status are all latest-wins), which made LWW look sufficient.
  Two fresh features immediately exceed the kernel's fold vocabulary. **The kernel
  substrate (ids/HLC/event-log/kind-registry/status-contract/CM-registry)
  generalizes; the kernel's FOLD LIBRARY does not.** (§Kernel-gap.)
- **The live oracle (farmOS Docker @ localhost:8095) is DOWN and its control plane
  (OrbStack) is unresponsive** — `curl` hangs 29s, `docker ps` hard-hangs past a
  `timeout 25` kill. This BLOCKS recipe stage 4 (OBSERVE), the acceptance spine
  the entire 9h5 line rests on. No fixtures could be observed; the mined candidate
  semantics are therefore **UNOBSERVED** (source-derived, clearly flagged), and the
  build below is judged on **kernel-conformance only, NOT value-equivalence**. This
  is the most important wave-1 operational risk the pilot found: if the oracle
  flakes, the fan-out stalls with no acceptance signal.
- **Verdict: the recipe is NOT yet wave-ready.** The upstream stages
  (scope/surface/semantics/decide) are wave-ready and cheap. Three concrete
  blockers must close first: (1) the kernel fold-vocabulary gap; (2) `propose-adapter`
  does not consume the bound CM-decision registry, so it re-derived a
  birth-uniqueness mechanic that **conflicts** with the frozen kernel decision
  (min-UUID vs earliest-HLC); (3) live-oracle reliability + a no-oracle fallback
  policy. Full friction log in §Friction.

avoiding role/permission modules (per the differential: domain vocabulary
survives, permissions/idiom don't), candidates were scored on **real domain value
semantics the method names can't telegraph** and on **exercising kernel surfaces
the prior two features did not**.

|---|--:|---|---|---|:--:|

| **farm_animal lifecycle** | 6 (animal) + 38 (birth) | asset/animal + log/birth, 1.00 | birth-uniqueness (hard); mother appended to child **iff no parent**; grow-only nicknames | **CM registry (kernel-bound)** + non-LWW lineage folds | **w0b ✓** |
| farm_lab_test | 25 | log/lab_test, 1.00 | lab result value/units | LWW-ish (like logs) — redundant with built | no |
| farm_seeding | 4 | log/seeding, 1.00 | too thin (4 nodes) | little | no |
| farm_group membership | 0* | asset/group | latest-wins membership | **already the studied discriminator** (9h5.8/9h5.10) | no |
| sensor/data_stream | 334 | core mega-island (0.615) | reading aggregation | NOT a clean slice (core blob) | no |

*group/birth show 0 under a `file:`-prefix count; both are real modules (birth =
38 nodes by qualified_name). group was excluded as already-studied; data_stream as
non-clean (it sits in the 0.615-persistence core mega-island, violating the

**Why these two together:** they are the smallest pair that makes the kernel earn
its generality claim on *new* ground. w0a attacks the **fold vocabulary** (is LWW
enough?); w0b attacks the **decision registry** (does a kernel-bound decision flow
through the recipe as a fixed input, and do fresh features introduce new hard
decisions?). Both have value semantics no method name reveals (`getInventory`
hides reset-assignment; `recordBirth` hides append-if-empty lineage). Per the
9h5.10 lesson, both were scoped to their **read-authoring** modules, not just their
own island: w0a includes `quantity` (inventory reads quantity adjustment rows);
w0b includes `asset/animal` (birth writes the animal's parent/birthdate fields).

## Kernel-gap: the fold-vocabulary finding (the headline)

The kernel freezes exactly one projection fold — latest-wins — because that is what
diverged across the seven isolated builds of the *first two* features (membership
additive-vs-LWW, ce015be4). Two FRESH features immediately need folds the kernel
does not provide:

| feature | projection | fold shape | kernel primitive? |
|---|---|---|---|
| w0a inventory | `getInventory` | **ordered reduce**: reset **assigns**, inc/dec accumulate, over done adjustments since latest reset, keyed on (effectiveTime, HLC-tiebreak) | **NONE** — not latest-wins; must hand-roll |
| w0b birth | parent lineage | **guarded first-write**: append mother iff child parent-set empty (complete veto otherwise) | **NONE** — not latest-wins, not additive |
| w0b animal | nicknames | **grow-only ordered multiset** (G-Set) | **NONE** — grow-only, not latest-wins |
| w0b birth | birth-uniqueness | earliest-HLC-wins + demote loser (LWW-family) | partial — HLC ordering yes, but the "demote loser to observation" mechanic is not a primitive |

Three of four are non-LWW. If wave 1 lets each of 100+ builders hand-roll these
folds, it re-creates the exact divergence risk the LWW freeze was meant to kill —
one builder's running-balance rounds differently, another's grow-only set dedups,
a third re-evaluates append-if-empty on every replay. **Recommendation (for Duke):
extend the kernel fold library with three more sanctioned primitives before wave 1
— an ordered/reset reduce (`FoldReduce` keyed on effectiveTime+HLC), a grow-only
set (`GSet`), and a guarded-first-write register — each as construction-enforced as
`pickLatest` is.** This is the pilot's most load-bearing structural output.

## DECIDE stage — decisions made (kernel-bound reused + new, flagged for Duke)

Per Duke's blanket decide-for-me authorization tonight. Kernel-bound decisions are
FIXED inputs (not re-decided); NEW hard decisions are resolved with rationale +
reversal condition and flagged for morning review.

### Kernel-bound decisions reused as FIXED inputs (no re-decision)
- **id-scheme** (`prefix_replicaId~counter`), **HLC** total order, **status-gate
  mechanism**, **event-kind taxonomy** (inventory/birth kinds must be *registered*,
  not invented), **pickLatest comparator**, and the **CM-decision registry** — all
  consumed as-is by both features.
- **birth-uniqueness** (w0b): kernel-bound to *earliest-HLC-wins, loser demoted to
  observation*. The recipe correctly treats it as fixed. (But see F2: the SURFACE
  stage did not respect it — a tooling gap, not a re-decision.)

### NEW decisions — w0a inventory
- **w0a-1 · inventory-fold-semantics** — *decided-for-me.* Current inventory =
  ordered reduce over `done` adjustments with `effectiveTime ≤ asOf`: start at the
  latest `reset`'s value, then apply increment/decrement deltas at-or-after that
  reset. Rationale: matches the mined source semantic; deterministic. **Reversal:**
  an observed fixture showing reset/delta interleaving that this ordering gets
  wrong. **Flag: routine** — but it is the concrete instance of the kernel
  fold-gap; the fold itself is a *new kernel primitive* recommendation, not a
  per-feature decision.
- **w0a-2 · same-effectiveTime tie-break** — *decided-for-me.* farmOS sequences
  same-timestamp adjustments by **ascending entity ID**; the kernel FORBIDS
  ordering by id. Resolution: break ties on the **HLC** (kernel-legal, total,
  replica-deterministic). **Reversal:** an observed fixture proving the id-order is
  domain-observable in a way HLC cannot reproduce. **Flag: ⚠ product-feel (minor)**
  — changes an output only for two same-instant same-(measure,units) adjustments.
  **RESOLVED 2026-07-20 — Duke CONFIRMED the HLC tie-break** (elicitation review,
  MetaCoding-tkj). Oracle observation would now corroborate, not gate.
- **w0a-3 · currentInventory status gate** — *decided-for-me.* Add a
  `currentInventory: require-confirmed` row to the kernel `STATUS_CONTRACT`
  (pending adjustments are inert). Rationale: source filters `l.status = 'done'`
  (AssetInventory.php:287). **Reversal:** oracle shows pending counts. **Flag:
  routine** — pinned by source, mirrors location's require-confirmed gate; requires
  a one-row kernel addition (reviewed, per the status-contract discipline).

### NEW decisions — w0b animal/birth
- **w0b-1 · parent-lineage-append-if-empty** — *decided-for-me.* On a birth event,
  append the mother to a child's parent set **iff the child currently has no
  parent** (any existing parent is a complete veto). Not latest-wins, not additive
  — a guarded first-write. **Reversal:** oracle shows a birth *correction* can later
  set a parent. **Flag: ⚠ product-feel** — decides whether a second/corrected birth
  can ever assign parentage; source says no. (Kernel fold-gap: guarded-first-write.)
  **REVERSED 2026-07-20 — Duke chose correction-overwrites-parentage** (elicitation
  review, MetaCoding-tkj): the parent field is an `LwwRegister` keyed on HLC, so a
  later correction reassigns it. Correctability beat source fidelity; the reversal
  cost nothing because w0b was never built. `GuardedFirstWrite` is now UNBOUND.
- **w0b-2 · nickname-multiset** — *decided-for-me.* Nicknames are a grow-only
  ordered collection (append, order-preserving, no replace). **Reversal:** oracle
  shows de-dup or removal. **Flag: routine.** (Kernel fold-gap: grow-only set.)
- **w0b-3 · sex-enum** — *decided-for-me.* Sex ∈ closed {F, M} stored codes.
  **Reversal:** none expected. **Flag: routine.**

No NEW decision required weakening a kernel-bound decision; the only kernel
*conflict* found is a tooling gap (F2), not a decision.

## Build + Judge (kernel-conformance only — oracle down)

**Rationale for one build, conformance-only judging.** With the oracle down, no
observed fixtures exist, so a value-equivalence JUDGE is impossible this session
(faking observed values would violate the method's core rule — the pure-LLM cell's
"plausible and wrong" guess is the standing proof). One blind Sonnet builder was
run for **w0a inventory** — the richest fold-generality stress — instructed to
build ON the kernel and judged on: (a) does it build on kernel primitives, (b) do
the prevention gates hold (registered kinds, IdMinter, status gate — recipe step 7's
"no ad-hoc kinds, no ordinal ids"), (c) does it reproduce the source-mined
semantics on its own tests. This tests recipe step 6's kernel-integration mechanics
without a value claim. w0b was not built (one build fits the session/budget; its
decisions and surface are the deliverable).

**Result — the kernel-integration mechanics work; the fold library does not
cover inventory.** The blind Sonnet builder (inputs = kernel + generated surface +
unobserved semantics + profile; forbidden all other sources) produced a build that
**passes 11/11 of its own tests** (independently re-run: `bun test` → 11 pass, 0
fail) and, unprompted, confirmed every kernel prediction:

| kernel-conformance check | result |
|---|---|
| Event kinds registered (no ad-hoc strings) | **PASS** — one `inventory_adjustment` kind registered + `freeze()`d at construction; `EventLog.append` is the sole choke point |
| Ids from `IdMinter` (no bare ordinals) | **PASS** — replica-scoped handles for both assets and adjustment events; no integer id anywhere |
| Ordering kernel-legal (HLC, not id) | **PASS** — the builder *refused* the source's id-ascending tie-break (candidate n4 / the generated surface both say "adjustment ID ascending") and broke same-`occurredAt` ties on `compareHlc`, citing ids.ts's ban on id-ordering and cross-replica incomparability — an independent confirmation of decision **w0a-2** |
| Status via the contract | **PASS (with a flagged gap)** — routed through `passesGate(status, "require-confirmed")`, but noted `STATUS_CONTRACT` has **no `currentInventory` row** and named the gate locally as a stopgap — an independent confirmation of decision **w0a-3** |

**The fold gap, confirmed from the inside.** The builder had to **hand-roll** the
running-balance-with-reset as an HLC-ordered left fold (`reset` assigns, inc/dec
accumulate) because *"pickLatest/LwwRegister cannot express this: they collapse a
set to a single newest write and discard order."* Its own recommendation, reached
independently: *"recommend a kernel `foldOrdered(events, hlcOf, step, initial)` … or
a named `runningBalanceWithReset` reducer … leaving it hand-rolled per feature
reintroduces exactly the 'locally valid, globally divergent' risk the kernel exists
to remove (one build folding from 0, another seeding from the reset and mishandling
ties)."* This is the §Kernel-gap finding, produced blind by the builder, not by the
orchestrator.

**Judging caveat (honest).** This is a **kernel-conformance + self-consistency**
pass, NOT a value-equivalence pass — with the oracle down there are no observed
fixtures, so the 11/11 is "reproduces the source-mined intent on the builder's own
tests," not "matches live farmOS." The value-equivalence JUDGE (recipe step 7)
remains BLOCKED on F1. What this build *does* prove: the kernel substrate + the
generated surface are a sufficient, prevention-gate-honoring foundation a blind
builder can build inventory on — and that doing so surfaces the exact fold gap
independently.

## Friction log — every manual intervention the recipe needed (wave-1 automation backlog)

Ordered by severity. This is the primary deliverable.

- **F1 · Live oracle down, control plane unresponsive (BLOCKER).** farmOS Docker @
  localhost:8095 unreachable (curl hangs 29s → http 000); OrbStack holds the port
  but the backing container is dead and `docker ps` hard-hangs past a `timeout 25`
  kill (uninterruptible). No Docker Desktop app; no colima. The OBSERVE stage — the
  acceptance spine — is fully blocked, and by the anti-poll-loop convention I did
  not burn budget fighting it. **Wave-1 action:** a health-check + auto-restart
  preflight for the oracle, AND a codified no-oracle fallback policy (this is bead
  9h5.19's regime); wave 1 CANNOT depend on a hand-restarted container.
- **F2 · `propose-adapter` ignores the bound CM-decision registry (HIGH).** The
  w0b surface re-derived birth-uniqueness as min-UUID/handle-tiebreak — a direct
  CONTRADICTION of the frozen kernel decision (earliest-HLC-wins). The SURFACE stage
  is fed the target profile but not the bound decisions, so it can propose surfaces
  that violate the kernel. **Wave-1 action:** feed `cm-decisions.jsonl` into
  `propose-adapter` and constrain generated convergence mechanics to the bound
  choice (or flag divergences).
- **F3 · Read-authoring scope drags in the adjacent module's own feature surface
  (MEDIUM).** Scoping `quantity` into w0a (needed for the adjustment reads, per the
  9h5.10 lesson) inflated the generated surface with 6 quantity-migration/
  rendering/revision methods unrelated to inventory. **Wave-1 action:**
  `propose-adapter` needs a "scope module X for reads only" flag so an adjacent
  module contributes read semantics without exporting its own mutators/surface.
- **F4 · Kernel fold vocabulary is LWW-only (HIGH — see §Kernel-gap).** 3 of 4
  fresh-feature folds are non-LWW and had to be hand-rolled. **Wave-1 action:** ship
  `FoldReduce` / `GSet` / guarded-first-write as sanctioned kernel primitives.
- **F5 · `ctkr` invocation friction (LOW).** First `python -m ctkr` triggered a
  full uv venv build (~min); the binary is `ctkr/.venv/bin/ctkr` after `uv sync`.
  A `curl` without `--max-time` and a bare `docker ps` each hung a 120s Bash slot.
  **Wave-1 action:** pin the ctkr entrypoint in the runner; always time-box network/
  docker probes.
- **F6 · mine-fixtures rank-1 can be a storage-idiom false lead (LOW).** w0a's top
  candidate by rank was `revision-lock/setNewRevision` (a Drupal revisioning idiom,
  not a domain value); the real value signal was the source-read lane (ranks 2-6).
  Consistent with prior findings (graph/CM rank ≠ port-value rank). **Wave-1
  action:** down-weight revision/rendering topics for the value pass.
- **F7 · Candidate `then` values are UNOBSERVED without the oracle (MEDIUM).** The
  mined given/when/then draft numbers are source-derived, not oracle-confirmed. They
  are good design intent but MUST NOT be scored as fixtures. Folded into F1.

## Wave-readiness verdict

**NOT wave-ready yet — but the gap is small, specific, and mostly tooling.** The
recipe's cognitive core (scope → surface → semantics → decide) is wave-ready: it
ran on two fresh features for $0.42, recovered the non-obvious semantics of both,
generated faithful surfaces, and surfaced/decided the new decisions cleanly. Three
blockers stand between here and a 100×-safe fan-out:

1. **Kernel fold vocabulary (F4/§Kernel-gap)** — add the 3 non-LWW primitives so
   builders don't diverge on reduce/grow-only/guarded folds.
2. **Surface↔decision coupling (F2)** — `propose-adapter` must consume the bound
   CM registry, or it will emit surfaces that contradict the kernel (it already
   did, on the very first fresh hard-invariant feature).
3. **Oracle reliability + no-oracle policy (F1/F7)** — the acceptance spine cannot
   be a hand-restarted container; wave 1 needs a preflight and a codified fallback.

Fix these three and the per-feature cost (~$0.21 of OpenAI + one blind build) and
the clean 4-stage upstream make the recipe genuinely wave-ready. The pilot's real
value was flushing them out: **two fresh features were enough to exceed kernel v1's
fold vocabulary and to catch the surface generator contradicting a frozen decision
— neither was visible on the two LWW-heavy features the kernel was validated
against.**
